Why Your Pillow Matters More After 40

As we age, the muscles and ligaments that support our cervical spine (the neck) naturally lose some of their elasticity. That means the position your head rests in for 7–8 hours matters far more than it did in your 30s. A pillow that's too flat, too high, or too soft leaves a gap between your neck and the mattress — forcing your muscles to compensate all night long.

The result? You wake up stiff, sore, and tired. Over time, this poor alignment can contribute to chronic neck pain, headaches, and even disrupted breathing (which brings us to snoring and sleep apnea — more on that below).

What the research says A 2024 review in the Journal of Orthopaedic Research found that pillow height and contour are among the most significant modifiable factors for cervical spine alignment during sleep — with misaligned pillows directly linked to morning neck stiffness and reduced sleep quality in adults over 40.

What Is the Derila Ergo?

The Derila Ergo is an upgraded version of the original Derila pillow — already one of the best-selling sleep products worldwide with over 2 million units sold. The Ergo version adds a unique butterfly-shaped contour design that serves two purposes: it cradles the neck and head for proper spinal alignment, and the wing-like sides allow you to naturally rest your hands under or beside the pillow — which many side sleepers do instinctively.

It's made from high-density memory foam that responds to your body heat and weight, distributing pressure evenly rather than creating hard pressure points. It's also designed to work for back sleepers, side sleepers, and combination sleepers.

The Three Problems It's Designed to Solve

1. Neck and Shoulder Pain

This is where the Ergo really earns its name. Traditional pillows — even expensive ones — leave the neck unsupported at the sides. The Ergo's contoured shape fills that gap, keeping your cervical vertebrae in a neutral position. In our testing, noticeable improvement in morning neck stiffness began within the first week. By week three, it was gone.

The memory foam also prevents the pillow from collapsing under the weight of your head (a common problem with standard pillows), which means consistent support throughout the night rather than just when you first lie down.

2. Snoring

Snoring in adults over 40 is extremely common — and often linked to airway position during sleep. When your head tilts forward or your neck is unsupported, soft tissue at the back of the throat can partially obstruct the airway, causing the vibrations we recognise as snoring. The Ergo's neck-support design keeps the head in a slightly more elevated, neutral position — which for many people reduces or eliminates positional snoring entirely.

This won't fix snoring caused by anatomical issues or severe sleep apnea, but for the majority of over-40 snorers whose snoring is posture-related, it can make a significant difference.

3. Sleep Apnea (Mild to Moderate)

Sleep apnea affects an estimated 1 billion people globally, and the over-40s are disproportionately affected. While the Derila Ergo is not a medical device and is not a substitute for a CPAP machine or clinical treatment, proper cervical alignment can reduce the frequency of airway obstruction events in people with mild or positional sleep apnea. The pillow keeps airways more open by preventing the head from tilting backward or to the side in ways that cause the tongue and soft palate to obstruct the throat.

Important note If you suspect you have moderate to severe sleep apnea, please speak to your GP. A pillow alone is not a treatment. However, for mild positional cases, improved alignment during sleep has been shown to reduce apnea events.
